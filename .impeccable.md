## Who this is for

XIIGen is an **AI engine platform**: it generates application flows (it does
not implement them itself) and it runs them. That produces three audiences
with entirely different density and chrome needs.

### Audience 1 — Platform operators (engine surfaces)

Roles: `platform-admin`, `platform-support`, `tenant-admin` when doing
technical work (flow authoring, reviewing arbitration verdicts, porting
decisions, deep-research monitoring).

Context: concentrated work, often at odd hours, sometimes when something is
wrong. They are evaluating state and making yes/no decisions. They do not
have patience for decoration.

Job to be done: **"Tell me what's happening, tell me what decides this,
let me act."** Not "use the dashboard" — the verb: *kill the stalled run,
approve the verdict, retry the porting cycle, promote the pattern*.

Tone: cold precision. Scientific instrument. Like a terminal that grew a UI.
Authoritative, legible, immediate. Not friendly.

Density: dense. Tabular-nums everywhere numbers matter. Operator screens may
show 30+ items before the first scroll. Chrome is visible because operators
navigate laterally between systems.

### Audience 2 — Business operators (tenant surfaces)

Roles: `tenant-user`, `tenant-admin` on business tasks, `event-organiser`,
`freelancer`, `business-partner`, `referral-user`.

Context: a quick stop during a busy day. They are running events, selling in
the marketplace, checking what they owe, looking at reviews. They may be
non-technical.

Job to be done: **"Did this work? What do I owe? What do I need to do?"**
The verb is *retry the payment, rsvp to the event, post the gig, accept the
booking, read the review*.

Tone: calm competence. Like a bank statement or a Stripe dashboard — clear,
unambiguous, direct. Not a social-media app.

Density: roomy. Business cards breathe. The FAILED invoice dominates PAID.
Chrome is present but quieter than the engine side — no ENGINE nav section.

### Audience 3 — Anonymous public visitors

Roles: `anonymous`, `public-marketplace-visitor`, `public-article-reader`,
`public-event-viewer`, `harmful-content-reporter`.

Context: a first touch. They arrived from a link or a search. They have no
account and no loyalty. They have zero context about XIIGen.

Job to be done: **one task**: *read the article, register, submit the
report, browse the marketplace, decide whether to sign up*.

Tone: zero learning curve. The screen should feel like a platform the
visitor already knows — Medium/Substack for public articles, Eventbrite for
event detail, Airbnb for listing browse, a trust-and-safety form for the
report. Not XIIGen chrome.

Density: kiosk. Single task, single primary CTA, no sidebar, no engine
navigation. Full-width reading column or centred single-purpose form.

## Brand personality

**Three words:** **legible, instrumental, unhurried.**

- *Legible*: every label a non-technical reviewer can read. Every number has
  a unit. Every state has a word alongside its colour. Nothing requires
  engineering context to understand.
- *Instrumental*: the interface is a tool, not a persuasion. It does not try
  to be friendly. It does not celebrate. It does not gamify routine work.
  It answers "what is the state?" and "what can I do?" in that order.
- *Unhurried*: no bounce, no elastic easing, no confetti on success, no
  purple-to-blue gradients, no glow. Motion is 150–200ms, ease-out, one
  property at a time. The product is calm under load — that is its
  signature feeling.

**Not these words, even though they tempt:**
- "Clean and modern" — absence of direction, not a direction.
- "Friendly" — wrong for the engine audience.
- "Minimal" — too easily executed as absence-of-design.
- "Data-driven" — every dashboard says this.

## Aesthetic direction

**Scientific instrument meets small-business calm.**

Shared token foundation. Density diverges per audience. Chrome diverges per
audience. Typography and color do not diverge.

### Reference platforms (use these, borrow conventions user already knows)

| Surface type                | Reference                 | What to borrow                            |
|----------------------------|---------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Topology canvas (engine)   | n8n, draw.io              | Three-column palette-canvas-properties    |
| Pipeline monitor (engine)  | Grafana, Vercel overview  | State counter bar as composition anchor   |
| Arbitration grid (engine)  | GitHub PR review          | Item rows × evaluator columns × verdict   |
| Feature registry (engine)  | LaunchDarkly flag list    | Card with state chip + decision actions   |
| Billing (tenant)           | Stripe billing portal     | Plan card + invoice list + Retry on FAILED |
| Marketplace (tenant/pub)   | Etsy card grid            | Price prominent + seller + state chip     |
| Booking (tenant)           | Eventbrite checkout       | Capacity bar + ticket selector + QR       |
| Checkout (tenant)          | Stripe Elements           | Order summary left, payment right, one CTA |
| Feed (tenant)              | LinkedIn feed             | Post card with author+timestamp+actions   |
| Registration (public)      | Linear / Airbnb signup    | SSO above email, one primary, footer terms |
| Article reader (public)    | Medium / Substack         | Zero chrome, article as h1, max-w-prose   |
| Harmful content (public)   | Trust-and-safety form     | Category tiles, textarea, no login        |
| Deep research (engine)     | Perplexity / Elicit       | Search input, source cards, synthesis     |

### Anti-references (never look like these)

- **AI dashboard template**: cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, hero
  metric (big number + small label), identical card grid repeated 6×,
  rounded rectangle with coloured side-stripe borders.
- **Linear / Notion warmth**: XIIGen's engine audience is debugging at 2am,
  not collaborating over coffee. Warm palette and playful type are wrong.
- **Dark mode by default with glowing accents**: that is "cool" substituting
  for a decision. XIIGen picks theme per audience context.
- **Bounce/elastic easing, staggered-reveal entry animations, sparklines
  as decoration**: all 2015 AI-generated product tells.

## Domain vocabulary (the color world, the signature, the words)

### Domain concepts (5+ from XIIGen's world)

1. **Pipelines** — stages through which work flows; each stage transforms the input.
2. **Arbitration** — multiple evaluators reaching a consensus verdict.
3. **Convergence** — a system iterating toward a better answer.
4. **Budget** — compute cost as a *live constraint* on a running process, not a static metric.
5. **Topology** — the structure of how parts connect, not just what they are.
6. **Signal** — weak information becoming actionable at threshold.
7. **Tracer rounds** — exploratory sub-queries that illuminate the search space.
8. **Promotion** — patterns proving themselves and getting elevated in the system.
9. **Scope** — tenant isolation boundary; an honored fence, never crossed.
10. **Flow** — a specific user journey across services (FLOW-01..48).

### Color world (5+ colors that exist in XIIGen's physical domain)

Not "warm vs cool" — what colors live in an ML-engineering room:

- **Oscilloscope green** — the color of a live, running signal.
- **Terminal amber** — old-school monitoring; warnings before they become errors.
- **Rack metal** — server-hardware neutral grey; functional, not decorative.
- **Deep navy** — a telescope looking at the night sky; oriented, vast.
- **Fault red** — a physical indicator-light red; means stop.
- **Parchment + ink** — for tenant surfaces: warm-white paper, deep-ink text. A bank statement surface.
- **Budget-strip teal** — the live green of a consumption strip that grades through amber at 70% and fault at 90%.

### Signature element

**The budget strip.**

A horizontal consumption bar *embedded in the pipeline node that is consuming
the budget* — not a separate metric card floating above the canvas. Green →
amber at 70% → red at 90%. A fuel gauge belongs on the dashboard of the
vehicle burning the fuel. This element is unique to XIIGen — it emerges from
the domain concept of "budget as a real-time constraint on a running
process" and would look odd on any other product. No generic AI dashboard
has it.

Companion signature: **the state counter bar** (Running / Complete /
Pending / Failed) at the top of every operational screen. This already
exists on FLOW-29 — extend it as a shared composition anchor across all
engine monitoring surfaces (FLOW-11 schema DAG, FLOW-14 ETL, FLOW-19
sagas, FLOW-26 meta-flow, FLOW-29 deep research, FLOW-33 bootstrap,
FLOW-35 arbitration, FLOW-45 history, FLOW-47 cycle extension).

### Defaults rejected (the 5 most important)

1. **Dark sidebar + white content area split** (the admin-panel template).
   → Same neutral surface throughout. Hierarchy through borders and
   elevation, not color-splitting.
2. **Identical card grid with rounded corners, same padding, repeated 6×**
   (the AI feature-card template).
   → Variable density. Operator cards dense and technical. Business cards
   roomy and scannable. Public cards borrow the reference platform's card
   shape (Etsy / LinkedIn / Stripe).
3. **Hero metric template** (big number + small label + supporting stats,
   as the billing MRR card currently does).
   → Metric-in-context: "$9.99/mo — FAILED — [Retry]" as one
   compositional unit. The number and the action and the reason live together.
4. **Cyan-on-dark "AI dashboard" canvas** (as FLOW-29 topology currently is).
   → Deep-navy canvas matching the sidebar color, not pure black. Node
   state communicated by border color **and** a text label (COMPLETE /
   RUNNING / PENDING / FAILED) — colour-only fails WCAG.
5. **Centered everything, kiosk treatment** (as CMS article, registration).
   → Left-aligned body text. Max-w-prose reading column. Asymmetric anchor
   on the hero action. Centered only when it is the one-task kiosk
   (harmful-content report form).

## Token vocabulary (tokens named from domain, not Tailwind defaults)

These are the names the product speaks in. If you read
`--pipeline-active`, you know what the product is. If you read
`text-green-500`, you know nothing.

### Text hierarchy (4 levels — never only 2)

- `--ink`           — primary text
- `--ink-muted`     — supporting text, labels
- `--ink-faint`     — metadata, timestamps, units
- `--ink-quiet`     — disabled, placeholder

### Surfaces (elevation scale — whisper-quiet shifts)

- `--paper`            — base tenant surface (warm-white)
- `--paper-elevated`   — card surface (subtle lightness shift)
- `--paper-overlay`    — dropdowns, popovers (one level above parent)
- `--rack`             — base engine surface (neutral near-black with navy tint)
- `--rack-elevated`    — node surface on canvas
- `--rack-overlay`     — side panel on canvas

### Borders (progression — not binary)

- `--border-quiet`     — barely visible, for section separation
- `--border-default`   — standard card edges
- `--border-emphasis`  — hover, active
- `--border-focus`     — focus ring

### Signal colors (functional, never decorative)

- `--signal-live`      — oscilloscope green, process is running
- `--signal-complete`  — neutral green, process finished successfully
- `--signal-pending`   — terminal amber, waiting on something
- `--signal-warn`      — caution amber, action advised
- `--signal-fault`     — indicator red, stop / failed
- `--budget-ok`        — start of the budget strip (green)
- `--budget-amber`     — 70% of budget consumed
- `--budget-red`       — 90% of budget consumed

### Brand accent (used rarely — rare is what makes accents work)

- `--brand-ink`        — deep navy, for the one primary CTA per screen
- `--brand-hover`      — hover state for the above

## Five design principles (every decision traces to one)

1. **Audience-scoped density, shared tokens.** Same type scale, same color
   tokens, same spacing unit across engine / tenant / public. What diverges
   is density (engine dense, tenant roomy, public zero-chrome) and chrome
   (engine full nav, tenant reduced nav, public nothing). Never "engineer
   tokens" vs "tenant tokens" — one system, three densities.

2. **State first, detail second.** Every screen shows the state before the
   detail. FAILED invoice dominates PAID invoices with a fault-red
   left-edge and additional vertical space. The Retry action sits beside
   the state word, not in an actions column at the far right. Action
   required rises.

3. **Domain signature lives on the node.** The budget strip is embedded in
   the pipeline node consuming the budget — not in a sidebar metric card.
   The state counter bar lives at the top of the page as a composition
   anchor. These two elements are shared across every operational screen
   and they are what makes XIIGen recognisably XIIGen.

4. **Grammar, not template.** Every tenant or public page implements one of
   five grammars — PROGRESS_STRIP, VERDICT_GRID, CARD_LIST with state
   badge, TOPOLOGY_CANVAS, KIOSK. A CRUD table backed by
   `/api/dynamic/xiigen-*` with Name / Status / Notes / Actions columns is
   **not a grammar**. It is development scaffolding left exposed. It never
   ships to a tenant or public audience.

5. **Zero learning curve for public.** An anonymous visitor borrows the
   convention of the reference platform they already know (Medium for
   articles, Stripe for billing, Eventbrite for events, Etsy for
   marketplace). The public surface does not have XIIGen chrome. It does
   not have a sidebar. The XIIGen wordmark is the only branding.

## The tests every page must pass before shipping

Run these **visually** on the rendered page, not in your head:

### The swap test (interface-design mandate)

If you swapped the typeface for a different one, would anyone notice? If
you swapped the layout for a standard admin-panel template, would it feel
different? The places where swapping wouldn't matter are the places you
defaulted.

### The squint test (impeccable reference)

Blur your eyes. Can you still perceive hierarchy? Is the action-required
item dominant? Is anything jumping out harshly? Craft whispers — borders
should not be the first thing you see.

### The signature test (interface-design mandate)

Can you point to five specific elements where your signature appears? The
budget strip, the state counter bar, the token names, the card composition,
the grammar implementation. "The overall feel" doesn't count — actual components.

### The AI slop test (design-for-ai)

If I told someone AI made this, would they believe me immediately? If yes:
count tells — Inter font, identical card grid, hero metric template,
cyan-on-dark, centered everything, side-stripe borders, bounce easing,
emoji-as-icons. 3+ tells = concern. 6+ tells = aesthetic direction has not
been applied.

### The token test (interface-design mandate)

Read the CSS variable names out loud. Do they sound like they belong to
XIIGen — `--signal-live`, `--budget-amber`, `--ink-faint`, `--rack-elevated`
— or could they belong to any project — `text-green-500`, `bg-gray-900`?

### The non-technical reviewer test (XIIGen-specific)

Can a person who knows nothing about XIIGen architecture look at the PNG
and answer YES to all three:

1. I understand what this feature does.
2. I can see what I should do next.
3. Nothing looks like it was left there by a developer.

If any answer is NO, the screen has not passed.

## Absolute bans (no exceptions, per impeccable skill)

These patterns never ship:

1. **Side-stripe borders on cards / list items / callouts** where
   `border-left` or `border-right` is greater than 1px. The single most
   overused AI design touch. Use full borders, background tints, leading
   numbers/icons, or no visual indicator at all.
2. **Gradient text** (`background-clip: text` combined with a gradient
   background). Decorative rather than meaningful. Solid color for text,
   weight for emphasis.
3. **Emoji as action icons** (♥ 💬 ↻ → 📧 etc. inside buttons). They are
   not aligned, not keyboard-accessible, not consistent across platforms.
   Use an icon set (Lucide / Heroicons) or text-only.
4. **Inter / Roboto / Open Sans / system-ui as primary body font** without
   a rationale that references this file. They are the AI default.
   (*This rule is applied incrementally — existing Tailwind setup keeps
   Inter for now, but headings on marketing-style surfaces and operator
   surfaces should move off Inter onto a chosen display face by
   FLOW-37 governance session.*)
5. **Provider-keys engineering banner** visible to any non-`platform-admin`
   role. Gate to platform-admin only. The banner is dismissable.
6. **Native `<select>` and `<input type="date">`** for styled UI. They
   render OS-native controls that cannot be styled. Build custom
   trigger + positioned dropdown components.

## Anti-references for this specific product

XIIGen must not look like:

- The generic n8n clone (we borrow the n8n 3-column layout but our
  topology canvas has budget strips and state counter bars, which n8n
  does not).
- Retool / internal-tool dashboards (too warm, too collaborative — wrong
  for the 2am operator).
- Linear (too startup-warm, too purple).
- Notion (too much chrome, too much decoration).
- A Stripe clone end-to-end (Stripe is the right reference for *billing*
  but the engine surfaces must be distinctly XIIGen, not beige).
- Any "AI-native" dark-mode-with-neon product.

  the page or capturing the PNG.

  that the finding violates. "Generic feel" is not a citation; "violates
  principle 3 — no domain signature on the node" is.

## What to update in this file

- Add a new reference platform when one flow reveals a new surface type.
- Add a new token when a new semantic color is needed (e.g. if we add
  `--signal-paused` for paused-but-not-failed nodes).
- Correct an anti-reference if the engine surface evolves into something
  specifically not-that.

Do not add "clean" or "modern" to the brand personality. Do not add
cyan-on-dark to the palette. Do not replace the signature with a metric
card. These regressions are what the rest of the file is defending against.
